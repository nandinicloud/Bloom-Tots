Child Development Guide

Understanding growth, milestones, and neurodiversity

The 4 Pillars of Growth

  • Physical: Moving from crawling to complex motor skills.
  • Cognitive: Learning, problem-solving, and logic.
  • Language: How a child communicates and understands words.
  • Social/Emotional: Managing feelings and building relationships.

Common Neurodevelopmental Disorders

Every child’s brain is unique. Here is a breakdown of common conditions:

Attention & Focus

ADHD

Focuses on struggles with executive function. Common signs include fidgeting, impulsivity, and difficulty finishing tasks.

Social & Sensory

Autism (ASD)

Impacts how a person perceives and socializes. Includes sensory sensitivities and a preference for routines.

Learning

Specific Learning Disorders

Dyslexia: Reading challenges.
Dysgraphia: Writing struggles.
Dyscalculia: Math difficulties.

Motor Skills

Dyspraxia

Affects physical coordination and motor planning. Often makes tasks like tying laces or drawing difficult.

Behavioral

ODD

Involves a persistent pattern of defiance, irritability, and arguing with authority figures.

Sensory

Sensory Processing (SPD)

When the brain struggles to organize information from the five senses (over-sensitivity or under-sensitivity).

Developmental Red Flags

Consult a professional if you notice:

  • No smiling by 6 months.
  • No babbling or pointing by 12 months.
  • No single words by 16-18 months.
  • Loss of any skills (speech or motor) at any age.
